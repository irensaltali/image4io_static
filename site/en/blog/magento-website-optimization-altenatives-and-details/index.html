Magento Website Optimization, Alternatives and Details

30 January 2021, magento ecommerce

Magento is a B2B and B2C online sales platform with two different versions, open sources and paid, used by more than 250,000 active customers. On March 31, 2008, a usable version was released.

Magento; has excellent functionality. It simplifies the work of those who want to set up an e-commerce site. And Magento is a great option if you want to open a trusted online store.

The biggest advantage of open source systems is that the community can contribute to improving the product. It also means that you can expand the functionality of your own site, either with your own effort or with the support of an expert.

When creating an e-commerce site, the first combination that comes to mind is usually WordPress and WooCommerce. However, Magento should definitely be learned and should not be overlooked.

Especially Magento has a free version that can be used by everyone. So if you are just starting out, your only cost will be domain and hosting fees. The Premium version of Magento is more enterprise-oriented.

Do you know that Magento, which stands out with SEO friendliness and its flexibility compared to its competitors such as WooCommerce, PrestaShop, OpenCart, and VirtueMart, is preferred by leading brands such as Nike, Samsung, Ford, Coca-Cola?

Magento is written in PHP and built on the Zend framework. Magento allows you to control the appearance, functionality, and appearance of the Site.

It also offers an intuitive interface and an e-commerce platform that offers rich marketing tools. Although it is not easy to learn Magento acquired by Adobe in 2018, you will not believe the functionality it has when you get used to it.

Magento1 vs Magento2

While Magento got a loyal audience with its original version, Magento 2 was launched to resolve some of the complaints that arose, addressing drawbacks. And he certainly made many improvements.

Performance and loading time

Magento 1

Performance is one of the biggest complaints found on Magento 1. Magento 1, websites have an average loading speed of over two seconds. Page loading speed is the most important factor affecting the conversion of the website.

Magento 2

Product loading and home page in Magento 2 is approximately 50% faster than Magento 1. It is also faster than Magento 1 in the payment process.

While Magento2 is better than Magento 1 in terms of performance, you will still need to constantly control the performance of your website in terms of SEO.

Mobile-friendly

Shopping on mobile increases significantly every day. By looking at this, not having a mobile-friendly site is unthinkable in this world. At this point, every e-commerce site must be mobile compatible.


According to Statista, mobile retail sales in the USA alone will reach $ 432 billion by 2022.

Magento 1

Mobile compatibility was one of the biggest problems found in Magento 1 after site performance. In the face of this problem, Magento developers have developed workarounds to make sites compatible with mobile devices, but they have not been very useful in the long term. The themes available on Magento 1 were not mobile-friendly.

Magento 2

Magento 2 is more mobile-friendly compared to Magento 1. Payment systems integrated themes for mobile have become more responsive.

Admin Panel

Both the performance of the website and the design and ease of use of the admin panel are important for keeping in proper communication with the customers.

Magento 1

The admin panel in Magento 1 was very slow and very difficult in terms of ease of use. It also had fewer dashboards than Magento 2.

Magento 2

Magento 2's admin panel is much more developed than Magento 1, with its advanced indicators and ease of use.

Recent orders, top keywords, best sellers, etc. many new controls are available in Magento 2' s panel.

Payment Gateways

Magento 1

Magento 1 supported many payment gateways. But it generally required a third-party integration.

Magento2

Apart from Magento 1, Magento 2 supports the most popular payment gateways such as PayPal, Authorize.net, and Braintree without any additional integration.

Security

Magento 1

Magento 1 no longer has any updates under the name of security. These updates and patches require expensive development costs.

Magento 2

With Magento 2, your site is as secure as you want.

Unlike Magento 1, there are updates and patches for security. However, you will need to manage your PCI compliance yourself.

How to increase Magento website speed?

With many customizable extensions in Magento, you can increase the speed and performance of your site.

High loading speed leads to the loss of important sales and potential customers.

First of all, you can click this link and find out your website speed.

Step 1: Use the latest version

Each update eliminates many problems. Magento 2's updates include performance improvements as well as bug fixes.

Step 2: Enable cache

Incorrect cache settings may cause your Magento site to slow down. Sometimes turning the priority off unintentionally unreliable 3rd party developers can access your site. This carelessness can cause your site to slow down.

You can easily turn back the cache back on by following these steps.

Firstly login into the Magento backend.

• Then Navigate to System → Cache Management

Then make sure all the cache options there are green and enabled.

Use CDN

Using CDN will significantly increase your website speed. Cdn is a network of interconnected and geographically distributed caches. It achieves high performance by distributing the existing service close to the location of the last user.

GZIP compression

You can also compress your website with Gzip. This process takes place on the server before the user enters the browser. You can use the following for Gzip compression:

·   HTML

·   CSS

·   JS

·   Fonts and texts.

Image optimization

One of the top 3 reasons your website slows down when you are not aware of it. You can test your website through Google speed test and if there is a problem with the image, you can start taking action.


By optimizing your images, you open your website faster and miss out on your existing potential customers and sales. There are many services and plugins such as image4io to optimize your images.

Use webP images

WebP is the newest image format. Png and jpg formats both take up a lot of space and are old fashions. That's why Google has developed WebP, a new generation image format specific to browsers.

For the visual optimization of your eCommerce Magento site, you can use tools that automatically convert the images on your site to Webp.

Can I use webp format on magento2?

Yes, you can. Magento supports Webp format images. It has been discussed for a long time whether it supports Magento and Webp. However, when there are extensions that convert existing images to Webp format for your Magento site, everyone made sure that they support Webp.

You can easily convert the format of your existing visuals to WebP, whether in a tool or manually. And you can easily use your Webp images on your Magento 2 site.
